<p><p>The Bi2Te3-based thermoelectric material market is segmented into "N Type" and "P Type" categories, based on charge carrier types. N Type materials, which have excess electrons, are characterized by high electrical conductivity and are often used in cooling applications. Conversely, P Type materials, possessing holes as charge carriers, enhance thermoelectric performance through efficient heat-to-electricity conversion. Together, these materials play crucial roles in thermoelectric devices, driving advancements in energy efficiency and sustainability across various industries.</p></p>
