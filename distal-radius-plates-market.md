<p><p>The distal radius plates market is primarily categorized into two types: volar plates and dorsal plates. Volar plates are designed for anterior fixation of distal radius fractures, allowing for optimal stability and wrist function during healing. In contrast, dorsal plates are positioned on the posterior side, providing an alternative stabilization approach for certain fracture patterns. Both types cater to varying surgical techniques and patient needs, contributing to the overall growth of the market in orthopedic surgery.</p></p>
